KYP.ai

KYP.ai is a process mining and analytic tool that gives you an overview of your processes and provides you with insightful data and recommendations on how to better your processes. My role as a Graphic and UX designer is very connected to the mission of providing a clean and easy way to visualize data and experience smooth navigation through rather complicated software.

As a UX UI designer am also involved in the ideation of new features with the product team from the phase of discovery and the realization of the documentation until the final QA process, managing the relationship with developers, and ensuring that the design language corresponds to the development language and that the programming process respects the business needs and times.

The revolution

The very first KYP.ai was developed/designed by the first group of founding developers. I fell in love with the idea of the product, how complex it was, and how these innovative ideas could help companies to better their processes and their incomes. I have also the opportunity my self to know more and more about business, automatization, and AI and being surrounded by such a visionary and innovative team with a solid background in business consulting and new development technologies.

One of the first projects that I approached was how to better a product with so much potential so that it would be easily understandable from every part of the organization and that could offer the best user experience and navigation. I decided to approach the project with a “revolution” collaborating with the product and development team.

1. Create new navigation
The old navigation had a very confusing and not congruent style. Following the business idea of concentrating the whole product on the concepts SEE, UNDERSTAND, CHANGE, I designed a top and left navigation with a search engine and the possibility to create your dashboard by opening new panels from the left menu. A new style was given to the whole navigation and more visual elements will now help the users to understand which part of the process they want to analyze.

2. Improve the layout
Another aspect of the product that needed a revolution was the general layout of the dashboard. Sometimes too much space was used unnecessarily and this was also creating problems or frustration in terms of usability on a 13inch laptop, which would have been a general screen size for our users. I decided to decrease the heigh of the automation bar and on the top and review alignment, layout, and padding inside the panels, to create general guidelines for future development.

3. Improve the color scheme
Not less important in this phase was to create a new color scheme to communicate to the user not only professional but also the emotional value of the data that they were going to visualize. I defined blue as the main color and created a new palette for the automation bar on the top assigning shades of magenta purple and pink to the recommender engine, green to productivity values, and shades of grey for data that we could not measure.

Design phases and styleguide

KYP.ai is a start-up that works with an agile scrum system. The phases to get from an idea for a feature until production, are several and the team is very focused to deliver fast, understand the problems and mistakes and fix them fast.

Phase 1 IDEATION
Ideation workshops, discussion of new ideas and projects, first low fidelity drawings of the new feature.

Phase 2 DEFINE
First mid-fidelity mockups and discussion with the product owner and business analyst regarding the value of the feature. Successively we will have a discussion with the production/development team about possible blockers and how we can develop the new feature, and what needs to be changed in the design.

Phase 3 PRODUCTION
High-fidelity mockups and prototype in alignment with the KYP.ai style guide. First development phase in which I normally attend several meetings with the developers to review the design even in the phase of production.

Phase 4 QA
Once developed the new feature will be reviewed by the QA and testing team. I am always involved in this phase too to check if and how the design works inside the product and its responsiveness. Most of the time this is a very good opportunity for me to learn how the product and the design system can be improved.

Phase 5 USER and TESTING
Since KYP.ai is a product in a very early stage of its life, we have had the opportunity to gather feedback from a future potential customer already in a very fresh phase of its development. When a new feature is created it would be run into a demo environment and presented by the sales team to the pilots. This is also a very important stage to understand the improvement potential of the product. In this phase, I am recently being involved in creating test methods and user research analysis like creating user personas, user journeys, and usability testing.

The styleguide
After the phase of the revolution, a very important thing for me to establish was the KYP.ai style guide for marketing and, most of all, product development purposes. I strongly believe that designers and developers should reach the point in which they “speak the same language”, and for this reason, I decided to develop a style guide strictly related to the way frontend developers use classes for CSS and angular components. I created a style guide in our shared documentation Cloud, connected to Figma, where the naming of UI elements and color would be the same used in development for classes, variables, and components, often aligning with the developers to find the right structure for it.
