7 Seas

7Seas is a weather website dedicated to all water sport aficionados, who want to plan a day on the water in a wise and safe way. With 7Seas user can search for new spots where to practice water sports, save them in their favourites, consult weather forecasts and the blog dedicated to water sports.

I realized this project as a student UX/UI designer from the user research part to the final UI design but, since a design can never be considered final without further testing, I would be open for more user testing and changes also after a potential development of the product. The biggest challenge for me has been how to visually organize pages which show a lot of information, such as the weather page and the blog.

Discover

01

Understanding the problem

The first thing I did to understand the problem of how to create a unique weather forecast website, was to analyze potential competitors such as Weesurf and Magicseaweed, weather application dedicated mostly to surf enthusiasts. After understanding which possibilities there are on the market, I developed some user and job stories, to highlight some potential needs of my future audience.

02

Interviews and surveys

After defining the goal of my research I launched an online survey and interviews, six potential users, in order to understand what people are expecting from a weather website in general. The information gathered were so broad and different, so I organized them through an affinity mapping method and decided which point should have been developed and which not.

03

Understading the user

The affinity mapping has been a strong tool to understand in which direction I wanted to develop the website and I could start creating user personas, Susi, Tyler and Gaetano. At this point, I decided to highlight only three main features of the product, one related to consulting the weather forecast, one related to research and saving of a spot and the last one related to the blog section. Around these main features, I developed the user journey maps and three different user flows.

Ideate

04

Sitemap

Once the three functionalities were defined through user flows I started with a first draft of the site map, but as I could expect, visualizing the map of the website in a clear schema helped me to understand that reaching the weather page of a location of accessing to the favourite page required too many clicks. A card sorting test with Optimal Workshop helped me to further understand the problem and develop a new sitemap, easier for the user to understand and use.

05

Wireframes from low- to mid-fidelity

With an eye on the last version of my sitemap, I began creating different wireframes, starting from the low fidelity ones, developing them with pencil and paper and with Balsamiq, then re-proposing the designs as mid-fidelity wireframes and going through the first loop of changes. At every step of the process and tried to step back and understand what could be hanged and what could be improved.

06

Prototyping

At this point of the process, I developed the designs of the three main features in a high-fidelity prototype. The aim was not to show a final, defined design of the product, but to understand if the navigation through the product was clear enough for potential user and if the three main features could be successfully fulfilled at this stage, before investing more time in a pixel-perfect prototype.

Test

07

Usability and A/B Testing

With a high-fidelity prototype ready for my potential users, I developed a test plan and conducted a usability testing with 6 participants, which were asked to complete 5 tasks through the prototype and think out loud. If the testing seemed frustrating at the beginning, I realised while conducting some affinity mapping and creating the rainbow spreadsheet, how much this helped me to improve the product and make it more user-friendly. When I was not sure regarding the layout and the colour choice of some pages, I conducted instead an A/B testing with UsabilityHub.com.

08

Refining the design

Thanks to the results of the usability testing I could start refining my design, stepping more in the UI phase of the project. I chose a grid system to apply to my design, redefined some elements basing them on the material design guidelines, which I personally admire very much, and developed a design style guide of the whole project in order to make a possible collaboration with other design easier and cleaner.

The final prototype

This exciting journey brought me to create a final prototype of the website for mobile since the whole project was deloped as a mobile-first design process, but a final desktop version would be contemplated in the final stages of the project too, in order to understand and put in practice the principles of responsive design.

what`s next

It is always hard to say when a design is finished or not, it would be always better, in my opinion, to do further test and test it again even after a one or two years to observe if people look at the design in the same way they did in the past.

To be more specific, I believe that the following part of the product could still be improved:

  • Organization of the favourite spots in groups
  • Organization of the accessible information in the news section
  • Avoid jargons in the location page

My plan concerning 7seas is to conduct more usability and A/B tests and keep on developing more functionalities if needed.
